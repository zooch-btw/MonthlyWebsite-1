Early Life:

Born in London, England on April 16th, 1889

When he was around 12, he appeared as ‘Billy” the page boy in Sherlock Holmes→became very popular

In 1917, he decided to become an independent producer for freedom and greater leisure in his movies

Made many great movies, such as:

  • A Woman of Paris
  • The Gold Rush
  • The Great Dictator

In 1966 he produced his last movie “A Countess from Hong Kong”

Died on Christmas day 1977, survived by 8 children from last marriage with Oona O’Neil and one son from his short marriage to Lita Grey


Major Accomplishments:

Charlie Chaplin starred in, wrote and directed some of the most memorable films in motion-picture history, including:

  • The Kid (1921)
  • The Gold Rush (1925)
  • City Lights (1931)
  • Modern Times (1940)--nominated for Academy Award as best actor
  • Monsieur Verdoux (1947)
  • Limelight (1952)

Significant Contributions to Society:

He was considered one of the best motion-picture makers of all time


Challenges he faced:

  • He had a tough upbringing in poverty
  • Father was an alcoholic and abandoned him at age of 3
  • Mother left unemployed, later became mentally ill and was sent to an asylum
  • Charlie was left nearly orphaned to fend for himself at a young age
  • Would tak odd jobs here and there just to survive, yet never lost his dream of being an actor
  • Barely learned how to read and abandoned formal education altogether at 13
